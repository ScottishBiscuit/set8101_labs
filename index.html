Introduction to Snake

Snake is one of the first video games that came from an arcade into a household name. It orignially came about in the 1970s and gained its popularity in the 1990s when it came bundled with the Nokia phones. The game is simple yet addictive, making it easy to pick up and play. It isnt complicated and just involves not colliding with yourself and eating the food to grow bigger.

Even though the game itself is basic, Snake became a cultural icon and introduced mobile gaming to the masses. It has remained popular over the years, with the game being recreated in various forms across different platforms. From a simple 2D game to modern 3D versions, the game has stuck to its roots while keeping up with modern tech.

Timeline of Snake's Evolution

  • 1976: The first iteration of Snake was released under the name Blockade by Gremlin. Gremlin was the two-player arcade game which introduced the core mechanic of a growing trail, laying the foundation for future versions of Snake.
  • 1980s: Snake came bundled with PC's like the Apple II and Commodore 64, their versions were different across the OS's but the gameplay remained the same.
  • 1997: Nokia introduced Snake to the masses by including Snake on its phones, starting with the Nokia 6110. It became one of the most recognised and played mobile games in history, appearing on over 350 million devices.
